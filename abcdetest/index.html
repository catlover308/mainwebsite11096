About Our Team

We're a new FIRST Robotics Competition team starting up for the 2026 season based out of Saugus High School. Our mission is to educate and empower students by exploring engineering, design, and teamwork through hands-on, competitive robotics: building real skills for real careers.

Even though we're rookies, our team is led by students with prior FRC experience who are also committed to staying involved after their time on the team, enabling us to build and sustain a competitive team from the ground up.

What is FIRST Robotics Competition?

The FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) is an international STEM program where high school students collaborate to design, build, and compete with custom-built robots.

Six-Week Build Season

Students gain hands-on experience in a self-led, internship-style environment building 115+ lb industrial-scale robots with limited guidance from adult mentors.

Real-World Skills

Students develop complex robots while gaining experience in fundraising, branding, and outreach - preparing them for industry careers.

Industry-Level Engineering

FRC requires highly customized designs, teaching machining, advanced CAD, and object-based programming - the exact skills used in manufacturing and engineering careers.

Tax Deductibility Information

All contributions made to Saugus High School and its affiliated robotics club are tax-deductible under Section 170 of the Internal Revenue Code. Saugus High School is a public educational institution, and as such, donations are eligible for tax deduction under U.S. federal law. No goods or services were provided in exchange for your donation, allowing it to be fully deductible as a charitable contribution.

Please retain related documentation for your records and consult your tax advisor to confirm the specific deductibility of your donation.
